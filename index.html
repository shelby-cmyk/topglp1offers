Frequently Asked Questions

What is a compounded GLP-1 medication?

Compounded GLP-1 medications are versions of semaglutide or tirzepatide produced by state-licensed 503A or 503B pharmacies rather than the original brand manufacturers. They contain the same active ingredient as branded products but are not FDA-approved. Compounding is regulated at the federal and state level, and any compounded medication must be prescribed by a licensed clinician for an individual patient.

Do I need a prescription?

Yes. All three providers on this list require a valid prescription from a U.S. licensed clinician, issued only after a medical evaluation. None of these programs ship medication without a prescription.

What are the side effects of GLP-1 medications?

Common side effects include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, and fatigue, particularly during dose escalation. Less common but serious risks include pancreatitis, gallbladder issues, and kidney problems. Discuss your full medical history with the prescribing clinician before starting.

Do I need insurance to use these programs?

None of the three providers on this list require insurance. All accept HSA/FSA payment, and all publish flat cash pricing.

What BMI do I need to qualify?

Standard eligibility is a BMI of 30 or higher, or a BMI of 27 or higher with at least one weight-related condition such as type 2 diabetes, hypertension, or sleep apnea. Final eligibility is determined by the prescribing clinician.

How fast will I get my first prescription?

Speed varies by provider. Thinnex averages under 24 hours from intake submission to clinician decision. Brello Health takes about 72 hours due to its longer onboarding. DirectMeds typically responds within 48 hours.

What kind of results can I expect?

Results vary significantly by individual. Published clinical trials of branded GLP-1s show average weight loss of 10–20% of body weight over 12+ months when paired with diet and exercise. Compounded versions are not studied in those trials, and individual outcomes depend on dose, adherence, lifestyle, and underlying health conditions.
